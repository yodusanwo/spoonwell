Section Header

Our Story

We are a husband and wife team, both U.S. Army veterans and both living with service-connected disabilities. Our time in uniform shaped everything about who we are — and so did what came after.

Like many veterans, we know firsthand what it feels like to be chewed up, spit out, and forgotten the moment you are no longer on active duty. That feeling should belong to no one.

That is why we created Spoonwell — a home where veterans are truly seen, respected, and cared for by people who understand the culture, the sacrifices, and the challenges that come with life after service.

"At Spoonwell, we are not just caregivers. We are veterans caring for veterans."

Pull quote as visual anchor. Keep copy personal and first-person throughout.
05 — About the Home / Core Values
Section Header

A Home Built Around What Matters

Small, intimate assisted living — 6 residents maximum. Located less than 10 minutes from Fort Benning, Georgia. Real care cannot happen in a crowd.

Icon

Community

With only 6 residents, Spoonwell feels like a household — not a facility. Residents eat together, spend time together, and look out for one another. The way it works in the military.

Icon

Fresh Food & Gardening

We grow food on-site and prepare meals using fresh, wholesome ingredients. Residents who are able are welcome to participate in the garden — a therapeutic, grounding activity.

Icon

Veteran-Centered Care

We speak the language. We understand the culture. We do not require residents to explain their service or their struggles. Everything we do is shaped by our own experience as disabled veterans.

Icons: recommend simple line icons. Suggested: two figures (community), a leaf/plant (garden), a shield/star (veteran). Alternatively, use small photographs from the home in each card.
